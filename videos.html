Sound & Mind - The Evolution of Rhythm Games

"Sound & Mind" is a video that I originally created as part of a game history project in the "Principles in Game Design" course at the University of Toronto Mississauga. I considered making it a public video ever since I came up with the idea, and after showing my final project to some of my friends, I decided to publish this video under the "The Pixel Polygon" brand. Rhythm games have played a large role in my life, but I find that to many other people, they're generally misunderstood. As such, I present this exhibit with an emphasis on music, not visuals.

Wii Would Like to Play - A Wii Retrospective

"Wii Would Like to Play" is a look back at the Nintendo Wii, released in 2006. This video focuses on the Wii from the perspective of human-centred technological innovation, and shows how it was able to compete in the market without the need for powerful hardware.

DOS Gaming on a Windows Laptop? - Fujitsu LifeBook 765Dx

After watching a video by "The 8-Bit Guy" on early DOS and Windows laptops, I decided to take my dad's (really) old laptop and take it for a spin trying out some retro PC games on it. After this, I was inspired to do this review on this particular notebook, and this video remains one of my most popular reviews to this day.
